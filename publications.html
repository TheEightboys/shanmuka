2025 IEEE

Low-Cost Quadruped Robot for Research Applications

Shanmukha Ram Peri et al.

This study was carried out to design and implement SmallKat, an affordable and versatile quadruped robot to address the limitations of existing educational and research robotics systems. While many universities offer robotics courses, the high cost and limited availability of advanced quadrupedal platforms constrain their widespread use. SmallKat is designed to fill this gap as it provides a comprehensive, low-cost solution that integrates kinematics, dynamics, controls, trajectory planning, and gait generation. The platform's modularity and robust sensing capabilities extend its applications to artificial intelligence, human-robot interaction, and social robotics.

2025 Springer Nature

A Simple Momentum-Transport Model for Sailing and Ram-Pressure Sailboat

Shanmukha Ram Peri et al.

This paper presents a straightforward momentum-transport model for sailing, specifically focusing on sailboats and their interactions with air and water. The model simplifies sailboat physics by assuming that the boat is powered by the relative velocity of the air and water, adhering to Galilean relativity. The primary focus is on sailboats' ram-pressure forces, which are responsible for redirecting fluid momentum. The model demonstrates that sailboats can achieve impressive dynamics, travel faster than the wind, and navigate upwind and downwind through tacking.

2021 Elsevier

Design and Analysis Using Aluminium 6061 for Lengthwise Gradient Honeycomb Structure to Obtain Graded Beam Properties

Shanmukha Ram Peri et al.

Honeycomb structures are natural or artificial structures that offer more strength and are low weight structures relatively. In this study, different types of hexagonal honeycomb structures are designed, and geometrical dependent properties are derived. The diagonal length of cells was varied from 12 mm to 21 mm and mechanical properties like total deformation were evaluated. On an average, gradient honeycomb structure deforms 15.43% less than the regular honeycomb structure.

2021 Elsevier

Design, Simulation, and Analysis Using 316 Stainless-Steel Alloy Based Automatic Protection Guard for Bike

Shanmukha Ram Peri et al.

An automatic protection guard for bikes reduces the impact to the side panels, engine, and the rider's leg when the bike collides with the ground, following the reverse quick return mechanism. Includes a tilt sensor to monitor the bike's lean angle to activate the system and a wave spring inside the hollow bars to absorb the impact. The average time taken for the protection guards to move from initial position to extreme position is 0.14 s, and the average deformation is 12 mm.

Hybrid Auxetic Materials Implemented in Crates & Non-Pneumatic Wheels for Shock Absorption

Shanmukha Ram Peri et al.

New hybrid auxetic structures have been designed, and modifications of the pre-existing auxetic & hybrid auxetic structures have been made to analyze the shock absorption capability. The basic hybrid auxetic structure consists of a bowtie structure partnered with a regular hexagon for stability. Modifications result in the enhancement of the Negative Poisson's ratio as compared to the basic hybrid auxetic structure, and they are also structurally more stable than their predecessor.
